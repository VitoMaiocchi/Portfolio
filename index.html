MARCHING CUBES

2022

This is one of the earliest projects mentioned here. Accordingly, it is also relatively simple. Nonetheless, I chose to present it here at the top of the list for the simple reason of its visual appeal.
This was one of my first experiments with OpenGL as well as the C++ language. The objective is to visualize 3D Perlin noise. To generate a 3D mesh made out of triangles from the Perlin noise function, I implemented the Marching Cubes algorithm. This algorithm samples the 3D function at discrete points and constructs a mesh from that. The different colors indicate the normal of the mesh or, equivalently, the direction of the noise function gradient.
